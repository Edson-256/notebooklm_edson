## Body — build it as a story, in pt-BR
1. **Quem é Orc (retomar a unidade 2):** Orc = the fallen name of Luvah, the Zoa of Passion/Energy; the spirit of revolution, red ("ruddy"), erupting against Urizen's cold law. Frye's title for him: the **thief of fire**, Blake's **Prometheus** — the giant of human energy kept chained under the earth by the sky-gods. Tie back to the myth map (unit 2): Albion, the four Zoas, Urizen the tyrant-reason, Los the imagination.
2. ***America* (1793):**
   - the **"Preludium"**: Orc bound in "tenfold chains", fed by the shadowy daughter of Urthona; he bursts the links and embraces her — the liberation of repressed energy (sexual/political/imaginative are one) = revolution beginning.
   - **Albion's Angel vs Orc**: the Guardian Prince of Albion (English power/law) accuses Orc ("Blasphemous Demon, Antichrist… transgressor of God's Law"); Orc's manifesto of holy energy — "That stony law I stamp to dust", religion scattered "as a torn book", **"every thing that lives is holy."** Revolution as apocalyptic energy against Urizen's law.
   - the **plagues recoiling**: Albion's Angel hurls plagues on America; American energy is too strong, the plagues **recoil** onto England ("the plagues recoil'd!") — leprosy on London's Spirit, plague on Bristol; repression turns back on the repressor. Then Urizen descends, "leprous", pouring his snows to cover Orc's red fire with cold cloud — containing the revolution "until France received the Demon's light" (point forward to *Europe*).
3. ***Europe* (1794):**
   - **Enitharmon's dream / the "eighteen hundred years"**: Enitharmon (Los's Emanation, the female will) sleeps and dreams for 1800 years = the whole Christian era figured as a long enchanted night ("Man was a Dream"), imposing the religion of repression ("Woman's love is Sin", "Forbid all Joy", "Thou shalt not"), the age when "god a tyrant crown'd."
   - **the coming of revolution to France / Newton's trumpet**: the spell breaks — a Spirit named **Newton** seizes the trumpet the Angel could not blow and gives the "enormous blast" (the cold science of Newton ironically heralding the end of the faith-tyranny); Enitharmon wakes "nor knew that she had slept"; Orc explodes again — **"in the vineyards of red France appear'd the light of his fury."**
   - **the hinge ending**: stress that *Europe* ends NOT with Orc but with **"Then Los arose"** calling his sons — Blake is already turning from the revolutionary agent (Orc) to the imaginative agent (Los). Flag this as the doorway to the rest of the audio.
4. **A leitura de Frye — o ciclo de Orc (the heart of the unit):**
   (a) Orc is Prometheus AND Adonis — thief of fire and dying-reviving god; revolution is **cyclic**, not random, like dawn and spring.
   (b) **THE KEY INSIGHT — the tragedy:** revolutionary energy rises against repression but **hardens over time into a new tyranny — Orc ages into Urizen**. Frye: the Urizen who defeats Orc "cannot be another power but Orc himself, grown old." The young rebel becomes the old oppressor; the serpent of revolution ends crucified on the tree of mystery. The very word "revolution" carries a tragic irony — it is part of "the revolving of life and death in a circle of pain."
   (c) the **seven Orc cycles** of history, moving Atlantis→Africa→Asia→Europe→back to Atlantis (America); *Europe* = the seventh cycle (Jesus → exhaustion 1800 years later) just as an eighth opens in America — and this time Orc also explodes in France. This is why the two poems are a pair.
   (d) **why Blake shifts from Orc to Los:** Blake watched the revolutions subside (Americans kept slaves; the wrong "Reason" was crowned in Paris); his one real change was to **reject the Orc-man as apocalyptic agent** — "no cause can triumph within \[the world] and still preserve its imaginative integrity"; Orc is bound to the cyclic wheel and can only renew an exhausted form, not open a new world; so we must look "elsewhere for the divine in man, for the pure imagination" = **Los**.
5. **Conexões e Para levar:** connect back to the myth map (unit 2 — Luvah/Orc, Urizen, Los) and forward to **Los in *The Book of Urizen* and *The Four Zoas*** (next units), where the hero stops being Orc (the fire that turns in the cycle) and becomes Los (the imagination that can break the cycle and open a new world). Tell the listener to read both poems asking two questions: *where is Orc chained and where does he burst free?* and *where, in the very triumph, is the fall already seeded?*
