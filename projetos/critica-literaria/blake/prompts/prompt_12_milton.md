## Body — cover, in pt-BR (explain, never recite)
1. **Felpham e Hayley:** Blake's three years at Felpham under the patron Hayley; the clash that turned the planned "slumber" into an ordeal; the result — Blake set *The Four Zoas* aside and engraved *Milton* and *Jerusalem*. The real quarrel becomes myth: **Palamabron** (Blake) vs **Satan** (Hayley / the rational "Selfhood").
2. **The "brief" epic:** Frye's distinction (after Milton) between the "diffuse" twelve-book epic and the intense "brief" epic modeled on the Book of Job; *Milton* is a brief epic — a single flash of illumination.
3. **O enredo (com a moldura):** the dead poet Milton, unhappy in heaven because in life he half-served the very "Reason"/moral law he should have fought, decides to descend ("to Eternal Death" = physical life) to **redeem his own error**; he enters Blake (through his foot); his one decisive act is to **let go of his "Selfhood"** — the proud rational ego — so that inspiration can live (frame this gently and clearly as the death of egoism); **Ololon**, Milton's separated Emanation, descends to be reunited with him in Blake's Felpham garden — the healing of division (tie to Emanation/Spectre from unit 2).
4. **O hino:** the Preface, "And did those feet in ancient time", and "I will not cease from Mental Fight... Till we have built Jerusalem" — explain it as a PROGRAM: an imaginative ("mental") struggle to rebuild the city of vision in this world; note it points forward to *Jerusalem*.
5. **A lente de Frye (3 pontos):** (a) two kinds of rebirth — **Orc** (life reappearing in a new form, the natural cycle) vs **Los** (the recreation of one vision by another); Blake "reincarnating" Milton is the Los kind — the same vision returning, purified; (b) for Blake, to imitate Milton is to imitate Jesus, because imitation = **recreation**, clarifying a vision rather than copying it; (c) *Milton* and *Jerusalem* form a **double epic** — prelude and fugue — *Milton* the individual prologue (one man overcoming his ego), *Jerusalem* the universal awakening.
6. **Para levar:** invite the listener — when the plot feels "absurd" (a star entering a foot), read it with the imagination, as the story of a man letting go of his pride so a truer vision can live; the question to carry: "what in me is the 'Selfhood' that would need to die so something truer could live?"
