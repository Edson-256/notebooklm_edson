## Body — walk the later Nights as a story, in pt-BR
1. **Onde estávamos / o fundo do poço:** Nights I–IV = the Fall; at the end of IV, Los sets the two limits (contraction, opacity = Satan/solid matter) so the fall can't become pure nothing. Frye frames V–VIII as the **Orc cycle**: birth, binding, "crucifixion" of energy, repeated era after era.
2. **Night V — Orc bound, the "chain of jealousy":** Los, jealous of his son Orc, forges a "tightening girdle... bloody cord" that hardens into iron — **"the chain of jealousy"** — and nails Orc to the iron mountain. Los repents, but Orc's limbs have **rooted into the rock** ("even to the Center"); the chain cannot be undone. Frye's point: pity/repression don't free energy — they **root** it (echo "Pity would be no more"). Urizen, lying in the deeps, hears Orc's howl and trembles.
3. **Night VI — Urizen explores his dens, the horror of Ulro:** Urizen wanders the "dens" (the physical world as trackless wilderness to the fallen mind); meets "three terrific women" (sinister trinity, Antichrist foreshadow). Core: the portrait of **Ulro** — the hell of pure abstraction, dead matter, reflected abstractions, solitude, the Selfhood walking a cosmos it has emptied of life. The coldest point of the poem, by design.
4. **Night VII — the TURN (the decisive Night; Blake rewrote it):** three faces of one turn — (a) Los **builds Golgonooza**, the city of art ("pillars high... Domes terrific"): art stops being a primitive seizure and becomes deliberate craft — Frye: the merging of **imagination and time** is "the axis on which all Blake's thought turns"; (b) Los **forgives his Spectre** — "I will quell my fury... in forgiveness of ancient injuries"; the Spectre weeps and proposes making **bodies for the dead** ("Let us Create them Counterparts"); Frye calls this the **"forgiveness of sins" = transferring the will from Selfhood to imagination** (the exact opposite of Night V's chain of jealousy); (c) Urizen's **"Tree of Mystery"** grows (religion/law, the Eden tree reread as the invention of sin), and under it Los beholds the **Lamb of God** "Clothed in Luvah's robes of blood descending to redeem." Note Frye's twin birth: as Los unites with his Spectre (advance → tradition of art culminating in Jesus), the Spectre + Shadow of Enitharmon produce **Rahab** (consolidation of error → Antichrist).
5. **Night VIII — war in heaven; crucifixion of Luvah-as-Christ; Vala/Rahab:** the antithesis sharpens into Incarnation vs. Antichrist. Crucifixion of Jesus and binding of Prometheus happen at once; **Rahab** (fallen **Vala**, the Great Whore, "Religion hid in War") presides; the Lamb-Luvah is slain. Urizen consolidates as **Satan**, the Covering Cherub. Frye on the closing lines of Night VIII: the ashes of Mystery re-animate — "they call'd it **Deism** And Natural Religion" — i.e., Antichrist reaches full clarity in **Blake's own 18th-century rationalism**, not in ancient paganism. Just before the Judgment, the gloomiest view: history as an endless cycle.
6. **NIGHT IX — the Last Judgment / Apocalypse:** the model for *Milton* and *Jerusalem*. Walk Frye's sequence: (a) revolution erupts like a self-protective jerk out of a falling-nightmare — the mind **wakes**; "Mystery's tyrants are cut off"; mankind reunites in brotherhood; (b) **Albion stirs and awakes** — asleep the whole poem (the poem was his dream), he wakes "with the sun in his eyes" and finds he is in his own home; (c) the great **harvest and vintage of the nations**: Urizen reaps the corn, Luvah gathers the vintage, Tharmas threshes, Urthona/Los grinds — the whole imagination made into **bread and wine**, the winepress and mill (instruments of torture in the fallen Nights) becoming the **communion feast** of reintegration; (d) **resurrection of the human form**: all creation pours out human life, the sea gives up its dead, animals/plants take on human character, Ovid's metamorphoses run backward; (e) the world made **human** again — "Man walks forth from midst of the fires: the evil is all consum'd," stars "consum'd like a lamp blown out," and the last line: **"The dark Religions are departed & sweet Science reigns"** (Science = Wisdom, Art and Science, the eternal life of a Man who is God).
7. **A lente de Frye (3–4 pontos):** (a) the theme = loss and recovery of the **identity of divine and human natures**; the barrier is belief in a nonhuman God founded on an objective nature, which Blake calls "Religion"; (b) the engine is **forgiveness, not revolution** — "no short cuts to vision through violence"; Los's patient art outranks Orc's eruption; (c) apocalypse is **waking to a real, familiar world**, not fleeing it — the darkening sun is the unreal vision disappearing; (d) **Frye's honest reservation**: Night IX may be an "effort of will" rather than the organic climax, beginning "with a bang" like a return of Orc, not the work of Los — which is why Blake **abandoned** the poem and restarted in *Milton*/*Jerusalem*.
8. **Para levar:** Night IX is the template — when units 12–13 reach the endings of *Milton* (self-annihilation, forgiving the Spectre) and *Jerusalem* (Albion's awakening), the listener should recognize this design: fall → division → Los's patient work → forgiveness → harvest/vintage → awakening. The whole arc was driving toward this reintegration. Anchor phrase to carry: **"in forgiveness of ancient injuries."**
