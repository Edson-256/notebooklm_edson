## Body — build the argument step by step, in pt-BR
1. **Por que Frye começa pelos dois menores textos:** *All Religions Are One* and *There Is No Natural Religion* (c.1788) are, in Frye's words, a summarized statement of the doctrines of the whole engraved canon, largely about Blake's theory of knowledge — so following Blake's own order means starting here. Before discussing what Blake *says*, understand from where he *sees*.
2. **O inimigo: Locke e o empirismo.** Locke = symbol (with Bacon and Newton) of every kind of evil/superstition/tyranny in Blake. State the empiricist doctrine plainly: mind born a blank slate (*tabula rasa*); an external, ready-made world out there; senses receive impressions; the mind stays **passive** and classifies them into abstract general ideas. Quote Frye on Locke thinking sight as "an involuntary and haphazard image imprinted on the mind through the eye by the object… the mind remains passive."
3. **A virada de Blake: a percepção é criativa/ativa.** The eye does not see — "the eye is a lens for the mind to look through"; perception is a **mental act**, voluntary. The man-as-perceiver is the **imagination**; "a man's imagination is his life." Tell the guinea-sun vs Hallelujah-Chorus sun example: the visionary sun is *more real* because more imagination went into perceiving it — not seeing more "in" it but more *of* it. Hence "a fool sees not the same tree that a wise man sees."
4. **A "ratio": o que os sentidos dão.** Walk through *There Is No Natural Religion*: first the adversary's chain ("Man cannot naturally Perceive but through his natural or bodily organs"… "limited to objects of sense") = Locke's man boxed in the five senses; then Blake's reversal — "Man's perceptions are not bounded by organs of perception. He perceives more than sense… can discover." Define **ratio** (Frye's term): the sum of mediocre common perception, the bounded, "the same dull round… a mill with complicated wheels." Senses give only the ratio (the bounded); the **imagination** gives the Infinite — "The desire of Man being Infinite, the possession is Infinite & himself Infinite."
5. **O Gênio Poético = o verdadeiro Homem.** From *All Religions Are One*, Principle 1: "the Poetic Genius is the True Man," and the body derives from it. Frye notes Blake used "Poetic Genius" for what he later simply calls **imagination**. Land the climactic line of *There Is No Natural Religion*: "He who sees the Infinite in all things sees God. He who sees the Ratio only sees himself only."
6. **Por que "natural religion" é o inimigo.** Explain the title: "natural religion" = the deist project of inferring God FROM external nature (the watchmaker deduced from the watch), a God built by reasoning out of "objective nature." Blake denies the root: if the senses alone are bounded and never reach the Infinite, no true religion can be "deduced from nature" — true religion springs from the Poetic Genius / Spirit of Prophecy. Hence "All Religions Are One." God is not at the end of a syllogism about nature; He is *within* the imaginative man ("God becomes as we are, that we may be as he is").
7. **A lente de Frye (3 pontos):** (a) **the passive mind is the enemy** — Locke's stamped, involuntary perception vs Blake's exuberantly active mind that "has realized its own freedom and understood that perception is self-development"; (b) **"Man is Born Like a Garden ready Planted & Sown"** — Frye's point is not innate generalizations but the denial that man is material formed by an external world rather than the *former/imaginer* of it; (c) **the three worlds** — memory / sight / vision = "the world we create, the world we live in, and the world we run away to": in memory we see nothing, in sight what we *must* see, in vision what we *want* to see. The visionary "passed through sight into vision."
8. **Tie forward + Para levar:** This is the seed of Frye's whole later criticism — he says interpreting Blake by Blake's own theory is "the beginning of a complete revolution in one's reading of all poetry" (foreshadow *Anatomy of Criticism*). Give the listener one reading rule: **always start from the primacy of imagination, never from "objective nature."** When he meets "Reason / Ratio / the bounded / Natural Religion / Locke / Bacon / Newton" → the passive mind; when he meets "Poetic Genius / Imagination / Vision / the Infinite / Prophecy" → the true Man who sees the Infinite and so sees God. Master this contrast and Blake stops being delirium.
