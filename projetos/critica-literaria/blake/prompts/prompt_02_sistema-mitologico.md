## Body — build the system step by step, in pt-BR
1. **A descoberta de Frye:** before *Fearful Symmetry*, Blake's prophecies were dismissed as the ravings of a half-mad mind; Frye showed there is **one coherent myth** behind everything Blake wrote. State this as the whole point of the audio.
2. **O Homem total (Albion):** originally one infinite divine Man = Albion = humanity = each of us; in Eden, man/God/world are one — reality is the body of the human imagination; there is no "outside".
3. **A Queda = divisão (não pecado moral):** Albion falls asleep, shrinks, and begins to believe in an **external, law-bound nature** before which he is small. The moment he accepts an "outside" he has fallen. (Link explicitly to Frye's attack on Locke/empiricism — the imagination abdicating: trading "I create what I see" for "I only receive what is out there".)
4. **Os Quatro Zoas** — explain each clearly and give the fallen name + symbol: **Urthona/Los** = Imagination (the smith forging in time); **Urizen** = Reason fallen into Law (the cold legislator, compass, iron book, "one law for the lion and the ox is oppression"); **Luvah/Orc** = Passion/Energy (revolution, fire, serpent); **Tharmas** = Sensation/Body (the chaotic waters). Tell the drama: at the Fall **Urizen usurps the throne**; **Los** keeps working inside the catastrophe, forging form so it doesn't dissolve — Blake's secret hero, the artist; **Orc** erupts against Urizen's law.
5. **Emanação e Espectro:** when a Zoa divides it splits into its **Emanation** (the feminine/creative/beloved part that separates — chief: **Jerusalem**, Albion's Emanation = liberty/the city of imagination; in the fallen world she becomes a distant nature-goddess, Vala) and its **Spectre** (the dry residue of rational selfhood — the defensive abstract "Selfhood"). Note that defeating one's own Spectre (self-annihilation) will be the decisive act of *Milton*.
6. **Os quatro níveis:** Eden (full vision) / Beulah (the gentle earthly paradise, dream, childhood Innocence) / Generation (our world of birth-and-death = Experience) / Ulro (the hell of pure abstraction, dead matter, Urizen's nightmare). Human life is climbing and falling this ladder; the Apocalypse is **Albion's awakening** — the four Zoas reintegrate, the Spectre is annihilated, Jerusalem returns, and "external nature" is revealed as the body of imagination. That is the ending of *The Four Zoas*, *Milton*, and *Jerusalem*.
7. **A lente de Frye (3 pontos):** (a) it is **one myth, and it is the Bible re-read** — Creation–Fall–Redemption–Apocalypse, but with the twist that the creation of the material world is itself part of the Fall, and redemption is man **waking up**, not being saved from outside; (b) there is **no supernatural** — all deities reside in the human breast; the humanist, imaginative reading of religion; (c) the system serves **reading**, not worship — knowing it is what lets you read Blake (and Milton, and the Bible) anew (foreshadow Frye's next book, *Anatomy of Criticism*).
8. **Para levar:** tell the listener to return to this map whenever a strange name appears in his English reading, and to listen two or three times until the four Zoas become familiar faces — after which the prophecies stop being delirium and become "one great poem about the fall and awakening of man."
