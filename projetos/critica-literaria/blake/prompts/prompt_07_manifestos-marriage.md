## Body — build it step by step, in pt-BR
1. **Why this is the hinge:** the three texts are the **manifestos** — first the two 1788 tractates (the philosophical base), then the doctrinal explosion of the *Marriage* (1790–93). Here Blake stops singing and argues: energy is holy, perception is the door to the infinite, the "evil" that morality condemns is repressed life. State the bridge up front: the holiness of energy = the Tyger (unit 5); the figure who will embody it in the prophecies = Orc, energy/"Hell" rising (unit 2).
2. **The two tractates (the base):** both attack Locke/empiricism — the idea that man is just a receiver of outer sense. **All Religions Are One**: the one-word thesis is the **Poetic Genius** = "the True Man"; the body derives from imagination, not the reverse; all religions share one source (each nation's reception of the Poetic Genius, "the Spirit of Prophecy"), hence all are one — no revelation from an external sky. **There Is No Natural Religion**: Blake dismantles a religion deduced from sense and reason; the key term is the **"ratio"** — reason as the calculus of the already-known, the "same dull round," "a mill with complicated wheels"; against it: "Man's perceptions are not bounded by organs of perception... He perceives more than sense can discover," "He who sees the Infinite in all things sees God. He who sees the Ratio only sees himself only," and man's desire is **Infinite** ("less than All cannot satisfy Man"). Flag: the **ratio** is the embryo of **Urizen**.
3. **The *Marriage* — the satirical inversion of Swedenborg:** the title mocks Swedenborg's *Heaven and Hell* (which kept good and evil cleanly separated by a gulf); Blake instead **marries** them, and makes Swedenborg a target of irony ("the Angel sitting at the tomb"; "has not written one new truth"). **State the master-irony before anything else:** when Blake says "Hell"/"Devil"/"Energy" he is **on their side** — his "Hell" is the side of vital energy, desire, the body, that orthodoxy demonized; his "Heaven" is reason/restraint/passive obedience. He adopts the enemy's vocabulary and flips it.
4. **"The Argument" — Rintrah roars:** "Rintrah roars & shakes his fires in the burdend air" — Rintrah = the **prophet's wrath**; Frye names it: the *saeva indignatio* that begins the *Marriage*. The little myth: the just man driven into "perilous paths" while the "sneaking serpent" of meek hypocrisy takes his place.
5. **The doctrine of contraries:** "Without Contraries is no progression. Attraction and Repulsion, Reason and Energy, Love and Hate, are necessary to Human existence." Blake labels them as what "the religious call" Good/Evil — "Good is Heaven" (the passive that obeys Reason) / "Evil is Hell" (the active springing from Energy) — but he is exposing, not endorsing. His three counter-truths: man has no Body distinct from his Soul; **"Energy is the only life... and Reason is the bound or outward circumference of Energy"**; **"Energy is Eternal Delight."** Whoever restrains desire does so because his is weak enough to be restrained, and Reason "usurps its place" (link to Urizen usurping the throne, unit 2).
6. **The Proverbs of Hell:** explain them as **moral explosives**, not etiquette. Anchor four: "The road of excess leads to the palace of wisdom" (note Frye: written against the Greek "Nothing in excess"); "Energy is Eternal Delight" / "Exuberance is Beauty"; "Sooner murder an infant in its cradle than nurse unacted desires" (Frye cites this as the center of "evil is negative" — repressed desire is a death-impulse).
7. **The "doors of perception":** "If the doors of perception were cleansed every thing would appear to man as it is, Infinite" — and "man has closed himself up, till he sees all things thro' narrow chinks of his cavern." The tractates' doctrine at its peak: cleansed perception does not change the world, it **reveals** what it always was — the infinite is here, closed by our five senses. Cleansing the door is the artist-prophet's work.
8. **The "marriage" itself:** the thesis is NOT "swap Heaven for Hell" — it is that **both contraries are necessary** and must be **wed**. Reason alone is barren, energy alone is blind; orthodoxy's error was to **separate** them, exile energy, call life "Evil," and command obedience. Frye's gloss: Blake calls the guardians of virtue "Angels" (wrong but necessary to the game) and those who question the whole structure "Devils"; and Frye is precise — only this kind of contrariety, the living "Mental Fight," **progresses** (the war of tyrant and victim only repeats). Eden is an "eternal Valhalla of conflicts," not a heaven of stuffed lions.
9. **"A Song of Liberty":** the prophetic verse close — the Eternal Female groans, a "new born terror"/"new born fire" (revolution) rises before the "starry king" (embryonic Urizen), the king's "ten commands" are stamped to dust, and the chorus ends "For every thing that lives is Holy." Name it: this "son of fire" rising against the stony law **is Orc** — the energy you will meet in every prophecy.
10. **A lente de Frye (4 points):** (a) **evil is negative** — restraint, not act; "all Act is Virtue"; this is the philosophical floor of the *Marriage*; (b) **energy is holy and conventional morality is the enemy** — the attack on "State Religion"/"Old Nobodaddy," the distant legislating God who is only the shadow of the tyrant; "Everything that lives is holy"; (c) **only the contraries progress** — the creative conflict of Angels and Devils, the "Mental Fight," not the sterile cycle of tyrant and victim; (d) **the prophet sees, he does not predict** — "Every honest man is a Prophet"; "insight, not second sight" (foreshadow Frye's next book, *Anatomy of Criticism*).
11. **Para levar:** tell the listener to read the *Marriage* **inverting the sign** (when Blake says "Hell"/"Devil"/"Energy," he is on the vital side), to ask of each proverb "what repression is this the explosion of?", and to carry the bridge: the holiness of energy here is the Tyger of unit 5, and the "son of fire" of the Song of Liberty is Orc rising against Urizen's stony law (unit 2). The tractates gave the base, the *Marriage* made it a manifesto, and from here Blake stops singing and begins to prophesy. Close on Frye's three pillars: **evil is negative, energy is holy, and without contraries there is no progression.**
