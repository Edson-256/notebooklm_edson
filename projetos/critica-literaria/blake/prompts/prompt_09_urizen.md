## Body — tell it as a story (fall → division → the work of Los → the prison), in pt-BR
1. **The frame — Bible of Hell / parody of Genesis.** Read it with Genesis beside it on purpose: numbered chapters/verses, a mock "In the beginning", a seventh day. Blake's Urizen is closer to the **Gnostic demiurge** than to a benevolent Creator. Core thesis to plant: **for Blake, creating the material world was a mistake, not a miracle — Creation = the Fall.**
2. **Who Urizen is (brief anchor to audio 2):** the **Reason**, one of the Four Zoas; fallen, he is the cold legislator, the "Ancient of Days" with the compass and the iron book. Name-echo: "your reason" / Greek *horizein* = to set limits (horizon).
3. **Opening scene — the self-closing shadow:** Urizen rises in Eternity "Self-clos'd, all-repelling", "a self-contemplating shadow"; he **abstracts** himself off into the **north** (the nadir of Blake's map). Gloss "abstracted": pulling the mind away from living reality and making it passive.
4. **The Book of brass — "One King, one God, one Law":** in his solitude Urizen writes a metal **Book of brass**, full of "laws". Quote the key line ("One command, one joy, one desire... One King, one God, one Law"). Warn against the pious surface ("Laws of peace, of love... pity, compassion, forgiveness"): for Blake **one single law for all life is tyranny** ("one law for the lion and the ox is oppression"; link to audio 5 / *London*). His true creation is the **rule**, not the world.
5. **What Urizen wanted — "a joy without pain":** quote "I have sought for a joy without pain, / For a solid without fluctuation." Explain via Frye: this is the **abstraction** that withdraws the divine mind from its creatures; the result is a "**world of solid obstruction**" — dead rock, ice, void = our fallen material world. **Therefore Creation already is the Fall.**
6. **The rupture:** the Eternals rage; "Eternity roll'd wide apart"; Urizen falls into the void he dug and roofs himself in a "petrific" womb of stone.
7. **Los enters — the smith who binds Urizen into TIME (the keystone):** Urizen is dissolving into formless nothing — "Unorganiz'd, rent from Eternity", "a clod of clay"; Los is terrified "at the formless unmeasurable death". So Los lights his furnaces and **forges chains of time**: "forging chains new & new / Numb'ring with links, hours, days & years." Over **seven "Ages"** Los gives Urizen a **body** and **measurable time** (each organ — spine, ribs, eyes, ears, nostrils, tongue, limbs — ends an Age, "and a state of dismal woe"). **The "ages of creation" ARE Los binding Urizen.** State the paradox clearly: creation is part of the Fall **and** Los's act is the **first redemptive shaping** — giving form so the fall does not dissolve into absolute nothing makes it **reversible**. The body and time are a prison, but a prison one can one day wake from.
8. **Pity, Enitharmon, Orc, the Net of Religion:** from Los's **pity** ("pity divides the soul") splits **Enitharmon** (Los = Time, Enitharmon = Space), wrapped in a curtain the Eternals call "Science"; from them is born **Orc** (energy/revolution/fire), bound to the rock with the **"Chain of Jealousy"**. Finally Urizen forges his instruments of measure (line, plummet, scales, **golden compasses**), breeds a dolorous race, sickens because "no flesh nor spirit could keep / His iron laws one moment", and from his sorrow spreads a cold web over the cities: "**The Net of Religion**" — abstract morality, "Thou shalt not", guilt — until men shrink, their senses close, they "rested on the seventh day... and forgot their eternal life." The parody snaps shut: the seventh-day rest = the moment humanity forgets eternity; creation has produced the prison.
9. **A lente de Frye (3 points, ch. 8 "The Refiner in Fire"):** (a) **Creation was a Fall, and the fall of a God as well as a man** — it happened when Albion "sought a pure or negative perfection", objectifying his power of abstraction = Urizen → "world of solid obstruction"; no external devil. (b) **Los is the hero — the refiner in fire**: the **divine smith**, "Blake's Demiourgos" (Creator of the universe AND worker for the people), who awakens the dead world to life as movement → creative time → life → imagination; the **furnace** refines fallen matter toward "the refined gold of the New Jerusalem" — and note Blake himself engraved with **metal and fire**, so the smith is the artist. (c) **"Time is the mercy of Eternity"**: binding into time only looks like a prison; it is **mercy**, because without time the fall would be "eternal torment", fixed and exitless — Los binds Orc/Urizen into the time of Generation precisely so that Regeneration becomes possible.
10. **Para levar:** read *Urizen* with two questions — *where does the parody bite Genesis?* and *who is the hero of this catastrophe?* Frye's firm answer: not Urizen who builds the prison, but **Los**, the smith who, hammering in the fire, gives the fallen world a **form that can one day be redeemed**. Creation is part of the Fall — but the smith's fire is already lit.
