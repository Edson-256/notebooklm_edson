## Body — build the map step by step, in pt-BR
1. **O que é o poema:** by ~1796 Blake set out to tell his WHOLE myth at once — the fall and resurrection of the universal Man, **Albion** — in one crowning epic (first titled *Vala*, then *The Four Zoas*). Never engraved, abandoned ~1807, left in a chaotic manuscript: Frye's "greatest abortive masterpiece in English literature." Key Frye point: **Albion stays offstage and asleep the whole poem** — what we watch is his dream ("a Dream of Nine Nights"), which is why it feels oniric and unexplained.
2. **O elenco — the four Zoas + their Emanations:** present as a clear cast. **Urthona/Los — Enitharmon**; **Urizen — Ahania**; **Luvah/Orc — Vala**; **Tharmas — Enion**. Explain Frye's two reading aids: Urthona is the eternal name of Los, Luvah the eternal name of Orc (same power, two names); and in this poem the Emanations "do practically nothing but wail" — they are the mourning of the fallen world, not plot engines.
3. **A estrutura — the nine Nights skeleton:** Frye says the plan "is not difficult to follow." Give the bird's-eye: Nights I–IV = the FALL (this unit); V–VII = human history / the Orc cycle / Los merging with the Spectre of Urthona; VIII = the crisis (Incarnation of Christ vs. the epiphany of Antichrist/Rahab); IX = the apocalypse. Say plainly that V–IX are unit 11.
4. **Night I — a primeira divisão:** the Fall begins in Beulah, in water, with **Tharmas** (the power to create life at will — first privilege lost). He splits from **Enion**; from her are born **Los and Enitharmon** = time and space. Anchor images: the cry "Lost! Lost! Lost! are my Emanations"; Tharmas sinking and turning "the Circle of Destiny"; Enion weaving a web of life that gains "a will of its own perverse & wayward" = natural life, a web of mutual preying. Note Frye: Enion's lament is the epic's version of the *Songs of Experience* (the grief of lost innocence).
5. **Nights II–III — Luvah, Urizen e a Mundane Shell:** Albion calls Urizen (still his own brain) to reorganize the collapsing universe; forced to work toward an external nature, Urizen builds the cosmos as a geometric temple of "mathematical form" — Blake almost parodying the Newtonian universe ("triangular, right angled... Rhombs, Rhomboids"). Name the key image: the **Mundane Shell** = the material/Newtonian universe as the **architecture of the Fall**. In parallel, Luvah seizes the sun, Albion falls for **Vala** (the veiled "nature") and starts creating life through an illusory "outside" (sex, the female will). Frye's gloss: Luvah = the blood (life-in-time), Urizen = the bones (space) — the wine and bread of a torn God-Man. Then Urizen's structure "falls to pieces with an appalling crash" (Night III); chaos = Tharmas returns.
6. **Night IV — o Mundo de Ferro, os dois limites, e a intervenção:** Luvah and Urizen smashed, only **Urthona/Los** is left; Tharmas assigns him to rebuild Man — Los the smith forging form inside the catastrophe (the secret hero). Los sets the **two limits** (contraction; opacity = solid matter/death) so life cannot fall into annihilation: the Noah covenant — chaos now has a border — and humanity sinks to its weak "Adamic" form. This is our present world, the **Iron Age**, established "six thousand years ago." EMPHASIZE the intervention: the **Daughters of Beulah** "create Spaces lest they fall into Eternal Death," name the space **Ulro**, brood over it "in care & love," and close the "Gate of the Tongue" against the Spectre — the **Divine Family / Council of God** beginning to work from inside the Fall. The Fall is real, but mercy is already at work — the thread that runs to Christ in Night VIII.
7. **A lente de Frye (3 pontos):** (a) the system is **coherent — the chaos is only apparent**: the manuscript is a mess but the plan (Tharmas → Luvah → Urizen → Iron Age, then history, then apocalypse) is clear; this is the whole point of Frye's reconstruction; (b) the **creation of the material world is itself part of the Fall** — the Mundane Shell is the symptom, not the neutral stage; "the essential barrier… is the belief in a nonhuman God founded on the fallen vision of an objective nature"; (c) the Emanations are the **mourning**, **Los is the hope** — track Los's work and the Daughters of Beulah, not the wailing women; that is the thread unit 11 will pull.
8. **Para levar:** tell the listener NOT to read the poem line by line first — he'll get lost. Instead: hear this map, open Night I, and hunt the five landmarks — "Lost! Lost! Lost!", the **Circle of Destiny**, Enion's web with "a will of its own", Urizen's geometric **Mundane Shell**, and the **Daughters of Beulah** creating spaces by mercy. Those five are the whole Fall. Then he's ready for Night V — unit 11.
