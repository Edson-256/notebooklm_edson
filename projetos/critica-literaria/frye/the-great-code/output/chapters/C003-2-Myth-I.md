The intimate and inevitable relation between mythology and 
poetry, then, seems to be operating here to some degree as well. 
Perhaps the myths of the Bible should be read poetically, just as we

therefore subject to external criteria of truth and falsehood; poetry

example of the kind of thing that is always happening. In our 
language, the universal in the history is what is conveyed by the 
mythos, the shape of the historical narrative. A myth is designed not to 
describe a specific situation but to contain it in a way that does not 
restrict its significance to that one situation. Its truth is inside its 
structure, not outside.
