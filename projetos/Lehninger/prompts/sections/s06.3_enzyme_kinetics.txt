Focus on: Michaelis-Menten equation and its assumptions, Km and Vmax meaning, Lineweaver-Burk and other linear plots, types of reversible inhibition (competitive, uncompetitive, mixed), irreversible inhibition.
What changed since 1995: high-throughput screening of enzyme inhibitors, fragment-based drug discovery, PK/PD modeling integrating enzyme kinetics, residence time concept (drug-target binding kinetics matter as much as affinity).
Oncology bridge: competitive inhibitors in targeted therapy (imatinib competes with ATP at BCR-ABL), allosteric inhibitors (trametinib on MEK), irreversible/covalent inhibitors (osimertinib on EGFR, sotorasib on KRAS G12C), understanding inhibition type predicts drug behavior and resistance mechanisms.
Key concepts to emphasize: Km reflects enzyme-substrate affinity, inhibition type determines how drugs compete — directly relevant to understanding targeted therapy mechanisms.
