Focus on: four bypass reactions (pyruvate carboxylase, PEP carboxykinase, fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase, glucose-6-phosphatase), reciprocal regulation with glycolysis, Cori cycle (lactate-glucose between muscle and liver).
What changed since 1995: gluconeogenesis role in cancer cachexia quantified, tumor-liver metabolic crosstalk characterized, futile cycling between glycolysis and gluconeogenesis in cancer.
Oncology bridge: cancer cachexia (affects 50-80% of advanced cancer patients) driven partly by increased hepatic gluconeogenesis fueled by tumor lactate via Cori cycle — massive energy drain on host. FBP1 loss in some cancers removes gluconeogenic brake.
Key concepts to emphasize: gluconeogenesis as energy-expensive process (6 ATP equivalents per glucose), Cori cycle as tumor-host metabolic parasitism, cachexia as systemic metabolic consequence of cancer.
