Focus on: monosaccharide structure (aldoses vs ketoses, D and L forms), ring forms (pyranose, furanose), anomeric carbon and mutarotation, glycosidic bonds, common disaccharides (sucrose, lactose, maltose).

Oncology bridge: glucose avidity of tumors is the basis of PET-FDG imaging — the most important staging tool in surgical oncology, Warburg effect (aerobic glycolysis), cancer cells upregulate glucose transporters (GLUT1), fructose metabolism reprogramming in hepatocellular carcinoma.
Key concepts to emphasize: FDG-PET exploits cancer's glucose hunger, understanding sugar chemistry explains why FDG works as a diagnostic tool.
