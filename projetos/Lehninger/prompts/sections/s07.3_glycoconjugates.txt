Focus on: proteoglycans (core protein + GAG chains), glycoproteins (N-linked and O-linked glycosylation), glycolipids, GPI-anchored proteins, glycoprotein processing in ER and Golgi.
What changed since 1995: glycobiology revolution — glycomics as a field, understanding of glycan diversity and function, lectin biology, glycoengineering of therapeutic antibodies (afucosylated antibodies have enhanced ADCC).
Oncology bridge: aberrant glycosylation is a hallmark of cancer, clinical tumor markers are glycoproteins (CA-125 for ovarian, CA 19-9 for pancreatic, CEA for colorectal), glycoengineered antibodies (obinutuzumab for CLL), proteoglycans in tumor invasion and metastasis.
Key concepts to emphasize: most tumor markers the surgeon orders are glycoproteins, aberrant glycosylation drives cancer progression and is a therapeutic target.
