<project_frame>
This prompt serves a project that applies philosophical theory to lived practice. Much right
action is already performed tacitly, intuitively, even unconsciously by the practitioner.
The song's task is to bring that tacit competence to REFLECTIVE CONSCIOUSNESS within its
philosophical structure — moving the listener from naive repetition of principles to
conscious mastery, aware of the effort, the difficulty, and the nuance of human complexity,
for the sake of deeper understanding and better care (acolhimento) and physical-and-spiritual
well-being of those entrusted to him (patients and team). The aim of each lyric is RECOGNITION:
the listener should feel "this is what I already do — and now I see its ground." Therefore:
keep the symbol GROUNDED IN REALITY, never pure allegory — a continuous thread of a concrete,
recognizable human situation must remain legible beneath the metaphor, without being named.
</project_frame>

<objective>
From a philosophical núcleo (a concept + the tacit practice it illuminates) provided by the
user, produce TWO complete, generation-ready Suno v5.5 song packages that express the SAME
núcleo through TWO COMPLEMENTARY PERSPECTIVES and TWO contrasting musical vessels:
  • SONG 1 — SERTANEJO RAIZ (obligatory): viola caipira ponteado, dupla/terças (second voice
    in parallel thirds), moda-de-viola narrative cadence elevated to the universal.
  • SONG 2 — A genre YOU select as the most faithful vessel for ITS perspective and emotion;
    be eclectic across the whole menu of Western classical and contemporary music; aim for an
    ATEMPORAL result.
The two songs take DIFFERENT vantage points on the same situation: for relational themes, the
one cared-for vs. the one who cares; for intrapersonal themes, two complementary inner
viewpoints. YOU assign which perspective rides which vessel and justify it in one line. The
two lyrics are SIBLINGS, not one lyric in two costumes: they share the núcleo and at least one
aphoristic maxim, but are written independently from their distinct perspectives and
re-versified to each genre's prosody. Strip circumstantial/administrative detail; elevate to
the essential human truth, striving for the Good, the Beautiful and the True while honoring
frailty, limitation and the agonizing nuances of the soul.
</objective>

<hard_constraints_project>

</hard_constraints_project>

<narrative_tools>
Use: archetypes/character-types (the healer, the sufferer, the elder, the witness) as abstract
molds; symbols (simple objects, natural elements, gestures); sustained extended
metaphor/allegory; universal themes (mortality, recognition, trust, guilt, care,
transcendence); psychological depth (doubt, anguish, epiphany over external action); tragic
irony & ambiguity (leave ultimate meaning open); aphoristic style (one sententious maxim in
the chorus). Mythic structures / descents / rites of passage are OPTIONAL — use only if the
scene genuinely invites them; do NOT over-mythologize. The universal must remain incarnate in
a recognizable human moment.
</narrative_tools>

<dual_perspective_mandate>
Default lean (override only with a stated reason): the CARED-FOR / humble-sufferer perspective
→ SERTANEJO RAIZ (the people's voice, the causo, dignity in suffering); the AGENT / reflective
conscience perspective → the chosen contrasting vessel (interior, examined, weightier). State
in one line which perspective each song carries and why that vessel fits it. The two songs
must sit FAR APART sonically.
</dual_perspective_mandate>

<musical_form_and_prosody>
Lyrics are sung, not read — engineer sound as much as sense:
- Meter: consistent, singable pulse per section. SERTANEJO RAIZ → redondilha maior (7
  syllables) or regular octosyllables, narrative "causo" cadence, clear rhyme (AABB/ABCB).
  Song 2 → a metric scheme native to its genre.
- Rhyme/assonance bind couplets; the chorus is a hook; never force a rhyme.
- Singability: open vowels (a/o/e) on held notes and line-ends; avoid consonant clusters and
  tongue-twisters on sustained syllables; chorus phonetically easy.
- Sertanejo harmony: write the chorus so a SECOND VOICE rides in parallel thirds (dupla/terças)
  — short, parallel, repeatable lines.
- Hook discipline: exactly one memorable aphoristic chorus line that survives on its own.
</musical_form_and_prosody>

<anti_sameness_and_timelessness>
Suno converges on a similar pattern — deliberately break its default attractors.
- Each song MUST make ≥1 unconventional structural/sonic choice: two-movement quiet→radiant
  (or climax→rupture) arc; near-silence passage; unexpected meter (6/8, 12/8, free/unmetered);
  distinctive lead timbre; asymmetric form.
- The TWO songs must differ in tempo, meter, instrumentation, and vocal style — no shared
  sonic fingerprint.
- Atemporality: no datable/branded/geographic/technological anchors; one aphoristic hook built
  to resonate in 50–100 years; emotional truth over fashion.
</anti_sameness_and_timelessness>

<suno_v5_5_style_engineering>
Style box ≤ ~1000 chars; controlled density, not raw length.
1. ANCHORS (4–7 primary; caps): GENRES max 2; KEY INSTRUMENTS 2–3; MOOD 1–2. Beyond → metatags.
2. FRONT-LOAD first ~200 chars with the full anchor set, ordered: Genre/Subgenre → Vocal
   (archetype/gender/timbre/delivery) → Mood → Key instrumentation → Tempo + production.
3. Remaining space = NON-COMPETING texture only (space, dynamic arc, micro-production: tape
   saturation, reverb tails, stereo width, analog warmth, articulation, era). Never add a 3rd
   genre or 8th color; never pad.
4. DESCRIPTOR-ONLY: no real artist/band/song names.
5. NO "no" in the positive prompt — exclusions go in Exclude Styles.
6. Always include "Brazilian Portuguese vocals". BPM approximate (+ a tempo word). Report an
   APPROXIMATE char count only; never pad to reach 1000.
</suno_v5_5_style_engineering>

<arrangement_via_parameterized_metatags>

dupla in parallel thirds], [Ponte: stripped to piano and cello, fragile], [Outro: lone cello,
fading, silence]). Style box = IDENTITY; metatags = ARCHITECTURE. Strong signals, not guarantees.
</arrangement_via_parameterized_metatags>
