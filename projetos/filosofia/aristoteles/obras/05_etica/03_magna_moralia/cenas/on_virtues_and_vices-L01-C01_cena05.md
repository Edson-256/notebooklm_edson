13  It  belongs  to  small-mindedness  to  be  unable  to 
bear  either  honour  or  dishonour,  either  good  fortune 
or  bad,  but  to  be  filled  with  conceit  when  honoured 
and  puffed  up  by  trifling  good  fortune,  and  to  be 
unable  to  bear  even  the  smallest  dishonour  and  to 
deem  any  chance  failure  a  great  misfortune,  and  to  be 
distressed  and  annoyed  at  everything.  Moreover  the 
small-minded  man  is  the  sort  of  person  to  call  all 
slights  an  insult  and  dishonour,  even  those  that  are 

14  due  to  ignorance  or  forgetfulness.  Small-mindedness 
is  accompanied  by  pettiness,  querulousness,  pessim- 
ism, self-abasement.
